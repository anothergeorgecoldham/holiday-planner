# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Travellers planning shared holidays with friends or partners. They are usually coordinating dates, accommodation, transit, booking references, and notes across devices, often in short planning sessions before or during a trip.

## Product Purpose

Holiday Planner gives small travel groups a clear shared calendar for trip logistics. Success means each person can quickly see what is happening on a day, update plans with confidence, and avoid losing details across chats, notes, and booking emails.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, travel-warm. The product should feel trustworthy and low-friction, with enough travel personality to feel inviting without becoming decorative or distracting.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page tropes, over-polished itinerary concierge aesthetics, scrapbook clutter, and heavy decorative effects that get in the way of planning. The interface should not feel like a travel inspiration site; it is a working planner.

## Design Principles

- Make the current trip and sync state obvious.
- Prefer clear, familiar product patterns over novelty.
- Keep travel detail readable on small screens.
- Treat collaboration and access as first-class, visible concepts.
- Preserve calm: errors, empty states, and auth flows should explain what happened without drama.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use sensible defaults: keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, readable contrast, reduced-motion support, and copy that does not rely on colour alone.
